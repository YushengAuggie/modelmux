# Naming Exploration for the LLM API Testing Tool

The product is a developer tool: fast, vendor-neutral, open source, and local-first. The strongest names here are not "friendly AI companion" names. They should feel sharp, useful, and slightly dangerous in the right way.

Domain availability is a directional guess only, not a live check.

## 1. Metaphor Names

- Why it works: It suggests signaling into a distributed, messy landscape and getting a clear response back.
- Domain guess: `beacon.app` maybe taken, `beacon.dev` likely taken, `getbeacon.com` plausible
- GitHub repo: `beacon`
- Tagline: `Signal any model. See everything.`

## Top 5 Overall

### 1. Probe
Best pure product name in the set. It is fast, technical, easy to say, easy to spell, and perfectly aligned with what the user is doing: probing an endpoint and inspecting the result.

### 2. ModelMux
This is the most accurate technical name. It sounds like infra, makes the multi-provider story obvious, and avoids sounding like a toy prompt playground.

### 3. Tarmac
This is the boldest brandable option. It has motion, edge, and launch energy without being another AI cliche, and it gives the product room to expand beyond simple prompting.

### 4. Relay
Clean, short, and versatile. It communicates transport across providers well and feels credible for an open-source developer tool.

### 5. TokenScout
A little more specific, but that is a strength if you want the name to immediately imply observability around spend, usage, and performance rather than just chatting with models.

## Opinionated Shortlist

If the goal is strongest product-market fit, pick `Probe`.

If the goal is best technical credibility, pick `ModelMux`.

If the goal is best brand with the most personality, pick `Tarmac`.
